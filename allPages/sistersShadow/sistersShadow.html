Life in my Sisters Shadow. 

JW Aguirre 

  Although I am mildly joking, every now and then when the comparison game arises, I always seem to lose. Starting at a young age I always viewed my sister as the one to never dissapoint, ranging from great organizational skills, to impeckable behavior. When viewing myself, at the time, I had horrible organizational skills, awful time managment, and not the best behavior. Although my parents did a great job of ensuring I felt equal to her, often times I would be my own worst enemy.

  Now, I am sure someone is wondering why I brought any of this up. Yes, I thought the topic was funny, but more importantly, to show progress within myself. As a kid, I always had poor management in everything, and many other unproductive traits. Years later,the switch flipped and everything changed. Not only has my management improved, but also my organization, behvaior, as well as overall attitude. At first, I felt I could never escape my sisters shadow.Now, I am able to view my sister for the smart but average softball player she is. None of this is to brag in myself, because quite frankly I am still not anyone special. But, this is encouragement to anyone who has unproductive traits, and wants to change it.

   Although I am a sixteen year old junior in Highschool, if you take anything away from this article, I encourage you to find something you are intrested and run with it. Taking an intrest in STEM and running has really helped me find motivation, and create goals to work towards. If you can find something you are intrested in, trust me when I say you will see major progress in your daily walk. So, with everything said, it appears I am finally making my way out of my sisters shadow.
